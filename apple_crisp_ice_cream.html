Apple Crisp Ice Cream

Yield: 950 grams
Preparation Time: PT1H
Cook Time: PT40M
Author: Blue M Ice Cream & Gelato

Ingredients

  • **For the Ice Cream Base:**
  • 450g 2% milk
  • 200g heavy cream (33%)
  • 90g granulated sugar
  • 60g corn syrup
  • 35g skim milk powder
  • 2.5g CMC powder
  • 150g ED Smith Apple Pie Filling (chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 20g homemade gluten-free crisp topping (see below)
  • 3g vanilla bean paste
  • Pinch of fine sea salt

Homemade Gluten-Free Crisp Topping

  • 20g Bob's Red Mill 1-to-1 Gluten Free Flour
  • 15g Rogers Natural Dark Brown Sugar
  • 10g unsalted butter, softened
  • Optional: 10g gluten-free oats
  • Pinch of cinnamon and fine sea salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). In a small bowl, mix together flour, brown sugar, butter, optional oats, cinnamon, and salt until crumbly. Spread on parchment-lined baking sheet and bake for 12–15 minutes or until golden and crisp. Let cool completely and break into small pieces. Reserve 20g for folding into the churned ice cream.
  2. Whisk together granulated sugar, skim milk powder, CMC powder, salt, and cinnamon in a bowl.
  3. In a saucepan, combine milk, cream, corn syrup, and dry mix. Heat to 75–80°C while stirring.
  4. Add chopped apple pie filling and vanilla bean paste. Blend with immersion blender until smooth.
  5. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container and cool rapidly to under 10°C.
  6. Refrigerate for 4–12 hours to mature the base.
  7. Churn in compressor ice cream maker until soft-serve consistency (approximately 35–45 minutes depending on machine and starting temperature).
  8. Fold in gluten-free crisp topping.
  9. Pack into a freezer-safe container and harden in the freezer at -18°C for 4+ hours before serving.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 95 grams
Calories per Serving: ~220

Notes

A nostalgic fall-inspired treat with warm cinnamon apple notes and buttery oat crisp. Creamier and softer thanks to a higher cream ratio and stabilizers. Store at -18°C in an airtight container. Best consumed within 10 days. Let sit at -13°C for 5–10 minutes before serving.
