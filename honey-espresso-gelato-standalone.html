Honey Espresso Gelato

Total Yield: ~600–700 g

  • 220 g whole milk
  • 180 g whipping cream (35% M.F.)
  • 100–120 g very strong, cooled espresso or mocha coffee
  • 100 g white sugar
  • 30 g honey
  • 2 g carboxymethyl cellulose (CMC)
  • Pinch of salt (optional)
  1. Brew strong espresso or mocha coffee and allow to cool completely.
  2. Pre-chill your ice cream maker (compressor or freezer bowl model).
  3. In a small dry bowl, whisk together sugar, CMC, and optional salt to prevent clumping.
  4. In a large mixing bowl, combine milk, cream, and espresso. Blend briefly.
  5. Slowly add the dry mix while blending to disperse stabilizer thoroughly.
  6. Add honey and blend again until fully incorporated.
  7. Chill the mixture in the refrigerator (covered) for 30–60 minutes.
  8. Stir gently to remove surface foam, then pour into ice cream maker with paddle running.
  9. Churn for 30–40 minutes until smooth and scoopable.
  10. Serve as soft-serve or freeze for firmer texture. Let rest 5–10 min before scooping if frozen.

Equipment Needed:

  • Hand blender or immersion blender
  • Ice cream maker (compressor or freezer bowl type)
  • Digital scale
  • Refrigerator
A silky, caffeinated gelato made with espresso, honey, and a hint of stabilizer for perfect texture.
