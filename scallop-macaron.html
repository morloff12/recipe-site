Scallop Macaron

Yield: About 15 macarons

Savory mustard macarons filled with scallop mousse, topped with ground cherry fluid gel and herbs.

Ingredients

Ground cherry fluid gel

  • Sunflower oil, for frying
  • 6 g shallot, sliced
  • 4 g garlic, sliced
  • 4 g ginger, sliced
  • 5 g Cointreau
  • 50 g pineapple ground cherries, husks removed
  • 25 g water
  • 5 g honey
  • 2.5 g apple cider vinegar
  • 0.75 g salt
  • 0.5 g citric acid
  • Agar-agar, 1.25% of total cooled weight

Scallop mousse

  • Sunflower oil, for frying
  • 14 g shallot, sliced
  • 10 g garlic, sliced
  • Salt, to season
  • 200 g scallop
  • 6 g whisky
  • 50 g cubed butter, cold
  • 50 g whipping cream, cold

Macaron shells

  • 67 g almond flour
  • 111 g icing sugar
  • 50 g egg whites
  • 28 g sugar
  • 0.5 g salt
  • 3.5 g mustard powder

Plating

  • Smoked sea salt
  • Borage leaves
  • Viola petals

Instructions

  1. Ground cherry fluid gel: Heat oil in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Add shallot, garlic, and ginger. Sauté for 8 minutes until translucent.
  3. Add Cointreau and cook for 1 minute. Flambé if you want.
  4. Add remaining fluid gel ingredients except agar-agar. Simmer gently for 30 minutes.
  5. Blend until smooth in a high‑powered blender.
  6. Pour into a container and refrigerate until cooled.
  7. Whisk cooled mixture with agar-agar in a saucepan. Bring to a simmer, whisking continuously.
  8. Pour into a container and refrigerate overnight until firm.
  9. Cut firm gel into rough pieces. Blend on high speed until smooth.
  10. Pass through a fine‑mesh strainer and transfer to a squeeze bottle.
  11. Scallop mousse: Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat.
  12. Add shallot, garlic, and salt. Sauté for 8 minutes until translucent. Do not caramelize.
  13. Add scallop and cook gently for a few minutes. Do not let it colour.
  14. Add whisky and flambé.
  15. When the whisky burns off, stir in butter and cream. Warm gently until butter melts. Keep below a gentle simmer.
  16. Remove from heat and cool to 45 C.
  17. Blend until smooth. Strain through a fine‑mesh strainer into a container.
  18. Refrigerate until cold, then transfer to a piping bag fitted with a #124 petal tip.
  19. Macaron shells: Sift almond flour and icing sugar together into a bowl.
  20. Whip egg whites until foamy and opaque.
  21. Gradually add sugar and whip to stiff peaks.
  22. Fold meringue and remaining shell ingredients into the dry mix. Macaronage until smooth.
  23. Pipe shells onto a tray. Tap the tray to release bubbles.
  24. Rest 20 to 40 minutes until a skin forms.
  25. Bake at 148 C (300 F) for 18 minutes. Makes about 15 shells.
  26. Plating: Pipe scallop mousse onto macaron shells in a steady back‑and‑forth motion.
  27. Season with a small pinch of smoked sea salt.
  28. Dot ground cherry fluid gel on top, about 5 small dots per macaron.
  29. Garnish with borage leaves and viola petals. Serve cold.
