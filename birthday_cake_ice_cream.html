Birthday Cake Ice Cream

Yield: 950 grams
Preparation Time: PT1H
Cook Time: PT40M
Author: Blue M Ice Cream & Gelato

Ingredients

  • 475g 2% milk
  • 210g heavy cream (33%)
  • 105g granulated sugar
  • 57g corn syrup
  • 38g skim milk powder
  • 2.4g CMC powder
  • 3 large egg yolks (~50g)
  • 4g LorAnn White Cake Batter Flavor Oil
  • 1g Watkins Pure Almond Extract
  • 40g Pure Food Rainbow Sprinkles (fold-in)
  • Pinch of fine sea salt

Instructions

  1. Whisk together granulated sugar, skim milk powder, CMC powder, and fine sea salt in a bowl.
  2. In a saucepan, combine milk, cream, and corn syrup with the dry mix. Warm while stirring until the mixture is steamy and the sugars are fully dissolved.
  3. Whisk the egg yolks in a separate bowl. Temper with some of the warm dairy, then return the yolk mixture to the saucepan.
  4. Cook, stirring constantly, until the custard thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon.
  5. Remove from heat and blend in LorAnn White Cake Batter Flavor Oil and almond extract with an immersion blender until fully emulsified.
  6. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container and cool rapidly to under 10°C.
  7. Refrigerate for 12 hours to mature the base and fully hydrate stabilizers.
  8. Churn in a compressor ice cream maker until soft-serve consistency (approximately 35–45 minutes depending on machine and starting temperature).
  9. Fold in rainbow sprinkles. Pack into a freezer-safe container and harden in the freezer at -18°C for 4+ hours before serving. Serve at -13°C for optimal scoopability.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 95 grams
Calories per Serving: ~240

Notes

Classic birthday cake flavor in a creamy custard base, boosted with white cake batter flavoring, a hint of almond, and celebratory rainbow sprinkles. Store at -18°C in an airtight container. Best consumed within 7–10 days. Let sit at -13°C for 5–10 minutes before serving.
