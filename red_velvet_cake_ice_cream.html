Red Velvet Cake Ice Cream

Yield: 950 grams
Preparation Time: PT1H
Cook Time: PT40M
Author: Blue M Ice Cream & Gelato

Ingredients

  • 450g 2% milk
  • 200g heavy cream (33%)
  • 90g granulated sugar
  • 60g corn syrup
  • 35g skim milk powder
  • 2.5g CMC powder
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 3g vanilla bean paste
  • 3g LorAnn Red Velvet Flavor
  • 1/4 tsp red gel food coloring (optional, for enhanced color)
  • Pinch of fine sea salt

Optional Cream Cheese Ripple

  • 100g cream cheese, softened
  • 40g icing sugar
  • 30g whole milk
  • 2g vanilla extract or paste
  • Pinch of fine sea salt

Instructions

  1. Whisk together sugar, skim milk powder, CMC powder, and salt in a bowl.
  2. In a saucepan, combine milk and cream and bring to 60–65°C.
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Temper with some of the warm dairy mixture, then return to the pot.
  4. Add dry mix and continue heating to 82–84°C while stirring constantly to form a custard base.
  5. Remove from heat and blend in vanilla bean paste and LorAnn Red Velvet Flavor with an immersion blender until smooth.
  6. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container and cool rapidly to under 10°C.
  7. Refrigerate for 4–12 hours to mature the base.
  8. Churn in compressor ice cream maker until soft-serve consistency (approximately 35–45 minutes depending on machine and starting temperature).
  9. If using cream cheese ripple, whisk together cream cheese, icing sugar, milk, vanilla, and salt until smooth and slightly pourable. Chill completely. As you pack the churned ice cream into a freezer-safe container, layer the ripple in between folds to create ribbons throughout.
  10. Pack into a freezer-safe container and harden in the freezer at -18°C for 4+ hours before serving.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 95 grams
Calories per Serving: ~240

Notes

A creamy custard ice cream featuring the nostalgic taste of red velvet cake with optional cream cheese ripple and vibrant red color . Store at -18°C in an airtight container. Best consumed within 10 days. Let sit at -13°C for 5–10 minutes before serving.
