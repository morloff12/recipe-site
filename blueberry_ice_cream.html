Blueberry Ice Cream

Yield: 950 grams
Preparation Time: PT1H
Cook Time: PT40M
Author: Blue M Ice Cream & Gelato

Ingredients

  • 500g 2% milk
  • 150g heavy cream (33%)
  • 90g granulated sugar
  • 70g corn syrup
  • 35g skim milk powder
  • 2.5g CMC powder
  • 120g fresh or frozen blueberries
  • 10g sugar (for blueberry reduction)
  • 3g vanilla bean paste
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Instructions

  1. In a small saucepan, combine blueberries and 10g sugar. Simmer for 10–12 minutes until syrupy. Stir in lemon juice and let cool. Refrigerate until use.
  2. In a bowl, whisk together granulated sugar, skim milk powder, and CMC powder.
  3. In a saucepan, combine milk, cream, corn syrup, and dry mix. Heat to 75–80°C while stirring.
  4. Add vanilla bean paste and blend with immersion blender until smooth.
  5. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container and cool rapidly to under 10°C.
  6. Refrigerate for 4–12 hours to mature the base.
  7. Churn in compressor ice cream maker until soft-serve consistency (approximately 35–45 minutes depending on machine and starting temperature).
  8. Fold in the cooled blueberry compote, creating streaks or swirls.
  9. Pack into a freezer-safe container and harden in the freezer at -18°C for 4+ hours before serving.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 95 grams
Calories per Serving: ~215

Notes

This fruity and vibrant ice cream combines a silky vanilla base with a swirl of sweet-tart blueberry compote. Store at -18°C in an airtight container. Best consumed within 10 days. Let sit at -13°C for 5–10 minutes before serving.
