Hawaiian Pineapple Ice Cream

Yield: 950 grams
Preparation Time: PT1H
Cook Time: PT40M
Author: Blue M Ice Cream & Gelato

Ingredients

  • 475g 2% milk
  • 210g heavy cream (33%)
  • 90g granulated sugar
  • 60g corn syrup
  • 38g skim milk powder
  • 2.4g CMC powder
  • 3 large egg yolks (~50g)
  • 120g pineapple purée (cooked and cooled)
  • 3g LorAnn Hawaiian Pineapple Flavor
  • 5g lime juice
  • Pinch of fine sea salt

Instructions

  1. Cook pineapple purée gently until slightly thickened and reduced to remove excess water. Cool completely and reserve.
  2. Whisk together granulated sugar, skim milk powder, CMC powder, and fine sea salt in a bowl.
  3. In a saucepan, combine milk, cream, corn syrup, and dry mix. Heat gently while stirring until steamy and fully dissolved.
  4. Whisk egg yolks in a separate bowl. Temper with some of the warm dairy, then return the mixture to the saucepan.
  5. Cook while stirring constantly until the custard thickens slightly and coats the back of a spoon.
  6. Remove from heat. Add reserved pineapple purée, LorAnn Hawaiian Pineapple Flavor, and lime juice. Blend thoroughly with an immersion blender until fully emulsified.
  7. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container and cool rapidly to under 10°C.
  8. Refrigerate for 12 hours to mature the base and fully hydrate stabilizers.
  9. Churn in a compressor ice cream maker until soft-serve consistency, about 35–45 minutes.
  10. Pack into a freezer-safe container and harden at -18°C for at least 4 hours before serving.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 95 grams
Calories per Serving: ~235

Notes

A bright, tropical ice cream built around real pineapple purée and boosted with Hawaiian pineapple flavor for clarity and consistency. Lime juice sharpens the fruit while the custard base keeps the texture smooth, creamy, and scoopable. Store at -18°C in an airtight container. Best consumed within 7–10 days. Serve at -13°C for optimal flavor and texture.
