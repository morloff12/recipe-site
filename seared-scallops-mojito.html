Seared Scallops with Mojito-Lime Gastrique, Mint Oil, and Potato Purée

Serves: 4

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 20 minutes

Ingredients

Scallops

  • 12 large dry sea scallops
  • Sea salt and white pepper
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (grapeseed or canola)
  • 1 tbsp unsalted butter

Mojito-Lime Gastrique

  • 1/4 cup white rum
  • 2 tbsp white balsamic or rice vinegar
  • 2 tbsp fresh lime juice
  • 2 tsp lime zest
  • 2 tbsp white sugar
  • 4–6 mojito mint leaves, torn

Mint Oil

  • 1/2 cup olive oil (light or extra virgin)
  • 1/4 cup mojito mint leaves (blanched)
  • Pinch of sea salt

Potato Purée

  • 400g Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1/4 cup warm milk (more as needed)
  • Salt to taste

Garnish

  • Fresh mint chiffonade
  • Microgreens (optional)
  • Lime zest or thin lime curls

Instructions

  1. Blanch mint leaves for 10 seconds, shock in ice water, squeeze dry. Blend with olive oil and salt until smooth. Strain and reserve.
  2. Boil potatoes until tender. Drain and dry briefly. Mash or rice and mix with butter and warm milk. Season with salt and keep warm.
  3. Simmer sugar and vinegar until slightly syrupy. Add rum, lime juice, and zest. Reduce by half. Add mint, steep 2 minutes, strain and keep warm.
  4. Pat scallops dry and season. Sear in hot oil 1.5–2 minutes per side. Add butter and baste during last 30 seconds.
  5. To plate: Spoon potato purée, place scallops, drizzle gastrique and mint oil, garnish with mint, microgreens, and lime zest.
