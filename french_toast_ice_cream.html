French Toast Ice Cream

Yield: 950 grams
Preparation Time: PT1H
Cook Time: PT45M
Author: Blue M Ice Cream & Gelato

Ingredients

  • 400g 2% milk
  • 200g heavy cream (33%)
  • 90g Rogers Golden Yellow Sugar
  • 60g corn syrup
  • 35g skim milk powder
  • 2.5g CMC powder
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 3g vanilla bean paste
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 tsp ground nutmeg
  • Pinch of fine sea salt

Maple-Soaked Toast Crumble (Gluten-Free)

  • 2 slices gluten-free bread
  • 15g unsalted butter, melted
  • 15g pure maple syrup
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Tear bread into small chunks and toss with melted butter, maple syrup, cinnamon, and salt. Spread on a lined baking sheet and bake for 15–18 minutes or until golden and crisp. Cool completely and break into crumbles. Reserve for post-churn fold-in.
  2. Whisk together sugar, skim milk powder, CMC powder, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt in a bowl.
  3. In a saucepan, combine milk and cream and bring to 60–65°C.
  4. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Temper with some of the warm dairy mixture, then return to the pot.
  5. Add dry mix and continue heating to 82–84°C while stirring constantly to form a custard base.
  6. Remove from heat and blend in vanilla bean paste with an immersion blender until smooth.
  7. Strain through a fine mesh sieve into a clean container and cool rapidly to under 10°C.
  8. Refrigerate for 4–12 hours to mature the base.
  9. Churn in compressor ice cream maker until soft-serve consistency (approximately 35–45 minutes depending on machine and starting temperature).
  10. Fold in maple-soaked toast crumble. Pack into a freezer-safe container and harden in the freezer at -18°C for 4+ hours before serving.

Nutrition

Serving Size: 95 grams
Calories per Serving: ~240

Notes

A nostalgic breakfast-inspired custard ice cream infused with vanilla, cinnamon, and maple syrup. Finished with gluten-free maple-soaked toast crumble for true French toast character. Store at -18°C in an airtight container. Best consumed within 10 days. Let sit at -13°C for 5–10 minutes before serving.
